Plasma-Treated PDMS: Wrinkle-Free Metal Films

O₂ Plasma Surface Modification PDMS Failure Analysis Process Development

Investigated O₂ plasma treatment of PDMS surfaces before metal deposition to suppress wrinkle and crack formation. Plasma treatment creates a stiff, silicate-like surface layer that reduces thermal/mechanical mismatch with deposited Cr/Au films. Eliminated cracking and wrinkling observed in untreated PDMS, enabling clean photolithography patterning and reliable lift-off for flexible sensor applications.

Photoresist Adhesion Optimization on Hydrophobic PDMS

DOE O₂ Plasma Failure Analysis Process Development

Systematic DOE (Design of Experiments) to solve photoresist delamination on hydrophobic PDMS. Tested 4 parameters: power (50-150W), pressure (200-600 mTorr), O₂ flow (40-80 sccm), time (30-180 sec). Identified optimal conditions: 100W, 400 mTorr, 60 sccm, 90 sec. Transformed complete pattern failure to clean, well-adhered resist features for flexible electronics.

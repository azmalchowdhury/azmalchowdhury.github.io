Cold Spray Deposition of Graphene Nanoplatelets on PDMS

Scalable Flexible Pressure Sensor for Wearable Applications

📄 Publication Status: Submitted to ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces
📅 Period: 2024 – 2025
🧪 Techniques: Cold Spray | Flexible Substrates | Sensor Fabrication
🎯 Focus: Scalable, binder-free conductive coating on PDMS

Project Overview

This project demonstrates a scalable cold spray approach for depositing graphene nanoplatelets (GNP) onto flexible PDMS substrates to develop wearable pressure sensors. Unlike conventional thin-film techniques, this method enables room-temperature, binder-free deposition, making it suitable for soft and stretchable electronics.

This work highlights a manufacturing-friendly alternative to conventional thin-film deposition, enabling rapid prototyping and scalable production of flexible sensors.

Key Highlights

  • Binder-free deposition of conductive graphene network
  • Compatible with flexible and soft substrates (PDMS)
  • Stable electrical response under mechanical deformation
  • Demonstrated wearable sensing capability

Performance Snapshot

  • Stable electrical response under repeated loading
  • Maintained conductivity under bending
  • Suitable for wearable sensing applications

Outcome

The cold-sprayed GNP films exhibited reliable electrical behavior and maintained conductivity under bending and compression. This approach provides a simple and scalable pathway for developing next-generation wearable sensors.
